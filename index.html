Carl Sagan

"We're made of star stuff. We are a way for the cosmos to know itself."

Carl Sagan (third from left) at SETI conference in 1971.

Here's a time line of Carl Sagan's life:

  • 1934 - born in Brooklyn, New York.
  • 1954 - M.S. degree in physics, University of Chicago; enters Ph.D. program in astronomy.
  • 1957 - first scientific publication, "Radiation and the Origin of the Gene," appers in the journal Evolution.
  • 1959 - begins consulting for the National Academy of Sciences(NAS) and NASA on planetary missions, which he continues to do for the rest of his career.
  • 1960 - receives Ph.D. in astronomy and astrophysics from the University of Chicago; his thesis title:"Physical Studies of Planets".
  • 1963 - joins the Harvard University faculty as assistant professor of astronomy.
  • 1968 - becomes director of the Laboratory for Planetary Studies at Cornell University in Ithaca, New York.
  • 1968 - marries Linda Salzman.
  • 1969-70 - collaborate with Linda on the design for a plaque carrying an interstellar message aboard Pioneer 10 and Pioneer 11, spacecraft destined to exit the solar system.
  • 1970 - begins work on Viking, a mission to land two spacecraft on the surface of Mars.
  • 1973 - first book, The Cosmic Connection, is published.
  • 1976 - receives appointment as David Duncan Professor of Astronomy and Space Sciences at Cornell University.
  • 1976 - is asked to devise a symbolic interstellar message to be carried beyond the solar system by the Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 spacecraft.
  • 1978 - the book The Dragons of Eden, a discussion of the brain and human intelligence, receives the Pulitzer Prize for general nonfiction.
  • 1980 - cowrites Cosmos television series (13 episodes) with Ann Druyan; the public television series would be viewed by more than half-a-billion people in 60 countries.
  • 1985 - publication of novel Contact, coauthored with Ann Druyan.
  • 1992 - Publication of Shadows of Forgotten Ancestors, second full collaboratiom with Ann Druyan.
  • 1994 - Pale Blue Dot is published.
  • 1996 - The Demon-Haunted World: Science as a Candle in the Dark is published
  • 1996 - dies at the age of 62.
  • 1997 - the film Contact, starring Jodie Foster, opens.
